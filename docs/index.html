Three separate model types

Weekly (Tab 2): predictor = that exact week's drought %. Each row is independent. Captures week-to-week variation.

Monthly (Tab 3): predictor = average drought % for that calendar month. One row per month. Smooths weekly noise.

Cumulative (Tab 4): predictor = average drought % from Apr W1 through that week. At Apr W1 = identical to weekly. Gets progressively more stable as the season builds.

How predictions update

1. Update data/drought_texas.csv with new weekly data
2. Run python process_data.py
3. New rows appear automatically in the relevant tables
Cumulative rows update every week · Monthly rows update when a new month begins · Weekly rows update every week

Why R² is relatively low (3–13%)

TX cotton abandonment depends on many factors: prices, insurance, planting conditions, localized weather. With ~25 overlapping years and a single predictor, 3–13% variance explained is expected. The CI is wide but statistically honest.

Season alignment

Drought weeks Apr–Oct of year Y → marketing year Y (Apr–Oct 2026 → MY 2026/27).

Cotton data notes

Marketing years 2004 and 2009 are excluded from training if USDA reports abnormal methodology changes. The model uses 15 minimum training years before making predictions.
